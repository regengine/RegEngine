# Industry Prioritization

The following table ranks the six industries by regulatory enforcement risk and the potential for monetizable compliance automation solutions, with 1 being highest priority. The ranking considers the severity and frequency of enforcement, data availability, and the commercial urgency/market size for compliance tools.

| Rank | Industry | Enforcement Risk & Automation Potential (Rationale) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Nuclear** | **Very High stakes.** Stringent oversight, catastrophic risk, rising enforcement. High automation potential due to complex documentation burdens and high per-plant budget. |
| **2** | **Energy** | **High scrutiny.** Broad impact (oil, gas, grid). Enormous fines (market manipulation, environmental). Large market size. High automation potential driven by new rules and dispersed operations. |
| **3** | **Aerospace** | **Strict safety.** Critical safety enforcement (FAA/EASA). Fleet grounding risks. moderate-to-high automation potential (SMS, conformance monitoring). |
| **4** | **Automotive** | **Significant demands.** Continual recalls/checks. Large scale (millions of units). High automation potential for supply chain and global regs. |
| **5** | **Food & Ag** | **Moderate-High risk.** Public health focus (recalls). Frequent but often smaller penalties per incident. Medium automation potential (traceability layers, fragmentation). |
| **6** | **Gaming** | **Increasing but niche.** Financial penalties (AML) and license risks. Niche-moderate automation potential; highly fragmented by jurisdiction. |
