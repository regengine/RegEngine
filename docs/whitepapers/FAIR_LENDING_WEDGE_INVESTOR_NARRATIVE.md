# Investor Narrative — Fair Lending Wedge

## Positioning
RegEngine is the compliance infrastructure layer that makes AI underwriting defensible under fair lending law.

It is not a dashboard add-on, bias calculator, or consulting wrapper.

## The Problem
- Underwriting teams ship model changes faster than compliance teams can defend them.
- Fair lending obligations are text-heavy, interpretation-heavy, and difficult to operationalize.
- When regulators ask for evidence, institutions scramble across spreadsheets and disconnected tools.

## Why This Wedge Wins
- **Regulatory urgency:** ECOA/FHA scrutiny is immediate and recurring.
- **High pain concentration:** one failed fair lending exam can block growth and trigger remediation costs.
- **Clear buyer:** model risk, compliance, and credit leadership already own this budget line.
- **Expandable graph moat:** once obligation-control-test-evidence links are in place, adjacent jurisdictions become additive.

## Product Thesis
Build a single system where every model decision risk can be traced to:
1. regulation,
2. obligation,
3. control,
4. test result,
5. immutable evidence.

That turns fair lending compliance from periodic audit prep into continuously defensible infrastructure.

## Current Product Surface
- Regulatory Intelligence Core for fair lending obligations.
- Bias and disparate impact engine (DIR/regression/drift/sensitivity).
- Model governance registry with revalidation deployment locks.
- Audit artifact generator with hash-verified export metadata.
- Executive risk summary API with weighted exposure scoring.
- Compliance Knowledge Graph linking obligation lineage to evidence and model version.

## Hard Scope Wall
RegEngine intentionally excludes:
- AML rules engines
- KYC onboarding workflows
- complaint case management
- vendor risk systems
- multi-industry expansion in the initial wedge

This concentration increases execution velocity and message clarity.

## Defensibility
- **Data-network effects:** repeated control/test/evidence cycles create proprietary supervisory memory.
- **Integration lock-in:** model change workflows and evidence pipelines become critical path controls.
- **Graph lock-in:** once institutions rely on traceable obligation lineage, replacement cost rises sharply.

## Go-to-Market Motion
- Land with institutions already deploying AI underwriting models.
- Start with one model family and one protected-class monitoring program.
- Expand account by turning regulatory requests into one-click artifact workflows.

## Success Metrics
- Time-to-evidence for regulator requests.
- Percentage of model changes blocked pending revalidation.
- Reduction in manual compliance report assembly hours.
- Exposure score trend improvement by model cohort.

## Narrative for Diligence
RegEngine is not selling fairness opinions.
RegEngine is selling the control plane that makes fair lending compliance reproducible, auditable, and machine-verifiable at model-change speed.
