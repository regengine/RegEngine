# RegEngine Investor Brief - FSMA 204 Focus

## One Line

FSMA 204 compliance infrastructure for mid-market food suppliers.

## Market

- 200,000+ US food facilities are in scope for FSMA 204 traceability requirements.
- Mid-market suppliers remain underserved by enterprise-priced traceability platforms.
- Retailers are enforcing supplier traceability readiness before the July 2028 federal deadline.

## Wedge

Free compliance tools (FTL Checker, Retailer Readiness Assessment) attract active buyers.
Each assessment receives founder-led review, turning product usage into high-intent pipeline.

## What Is Built

- Compliance engine: deterministic extraction and normalization
- Supply-chain graph: multi-hop recall analysis and impact traversal
- EPCIS 2.0 support: retailer-ready event interoperability
- Tamper-evident audit path: versioned, hashed, independently verifiable records
- Live free tools: top-of-funnel conversion and qualification

## Moat

Independent verifiability.

RegEngine does not ask customers to trust opaque dashboards. It provides records
that can be verified with external scripts and reproducible hash-chain checks.

## Business Model

- Free: FTL checking and basic readiness scoring
- Starter ($299/mo): CTE and KDE tracking, up to 100 products and 10 suppliers
- Professional ($799/mo): unlimited product and supplier coverage, EPCIS export, recall simulation
- Enterprise (custom): multi-facility rollout, API integrations, SLA terms

## Raise

$400K pre-seed to fund:

- 3 mid-market pilot deployments
- EPCIS 2.0 certification testing
- 1 additional backend/data engineer
- 12 months of operating runway

## Why Now

- FSMA 204 deadline is fixed and approaching.
- Retailer pressure is moving faster than regulation enforcement.
- Mid-market teams are actively searching for affordable infrastructure.
- Existing alternatives are implementation-heavy and priced for large enterprise budgets.
