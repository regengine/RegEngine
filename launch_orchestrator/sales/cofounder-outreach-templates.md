Hey [NAME] — I came across your work on [SPECIFIC_PROJECT/POST]. I'm building RegEngine, an API-first compliance platform for the FDA's food traceability mandate (FSMA 204). 200K+ facilities need to comply by 2028, and there's no mid-market API-first player.

The product is shipped and deployed — not a slide deck. I'm looking for a technical co-founder to own customer engineering and backend architecture. Meaningful equity, $400K pre-seed in progress.

Would you be open to a 20-min call this week? Happy to share the repo — the code speaks louder than any pitch.

## Screening Questions (For Initial Calls)

1. "What's the last product you shipped to real users, and what broke first?"
2. "If I told you our first customer meeting is in 2 weeks, what would you want to know before that call?"
3. "How do you feel about being on a customer call where they're frustrated with a bug you shipped?"
4. "What does 'co-founder equity' mean to you in terms of commitment level?"
5. "Have you ever worked in a regulated industry? What surprised you?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Wants to "redesign the architecture" before talking to customers
- Only interested if the raise is already closed
- Can't articulate why food traceability matters after you explain it
- Wants a guaranteed salary before equity vesting begins
- Has never shipped anything to production
- "I have 10 startup ideas" (you need focus, not more ideas)

## Green Flags

- Asks about the customers before asking about the code
- Has opinions about PostgreSQL performance at scale
- Says "can I see the repo?" in the first 5 minutes
- Has left a comfortable job before for something uncertain
- Asks about your worst day building this, not just the vision
- Gets visibly interested when you explain the 2028 deadline
